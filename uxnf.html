Case Study

Helping users help themselves

The Project

Nerd Fitness (NF) is a popular blog and fitness community that encourages users to build healthy habits. NF relies on a "Questing" system in which users form good habits to gather experience points.
The goal of the project is to redesign the Quest system to help users track the habits they are forming and guide them on their journey.
This project is not intended to be a superficial UI refresh. Structure and content are being redesigned as part of a complete exercise in improving the system usability.

This project is for my own exercise and enjoyment. I am not affiliated with Nerd Fitness, nor is this project.

Know Thy User

I began my project with a product analysis and competitor research. I used this information to create a survey for current active members of Nerd Fitness. I gathered responses from 72 users, including many additional comments and feedback from participants. You can see my survey and results here.

As a result of my research and user interviews, I compiled three primary user personas. The personas are based not only on the survey participant data but also on qualitative observation of users content on forums and social media.

Define & Design

The current Nerd Fitness system exists across multiple sub-domains and can be difficult to navigate. I started by tracing the existing architecture into a rough sitemap before reconsidering not only the page structure but the Questing system itself.
Ultimately, I broke down the Quests into two distinct types: Achievements, which are one-time accomplishments ("do 10 regular push-ups") and the new Quests, which are ongoing habits users try to form ("avoid soda for a week").
My initial design iterations are built on the distinction between Achievements and Quests, combined with a reconsidered architecture for user profiles.
