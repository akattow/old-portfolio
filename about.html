About Kat

My Work

I'm a left-brain/right-brain hybrid type. I'm also a self-taught front-end developer and user experience designer with a professional background in visual and graphic design.

The Crash

In April 2016, while working in graphic design and marketing, I crashed my moped on a pothole. I was extremely lucky to emerge from the ER without any permanent damage. The hospital and the subsequent months of specialists, oral surgery, and insurance questions was (unsurprisingly) a terrible patient experience.

When I needed help the most, I was confronted with a system that was nearly impossible to navigate. The medical staff assisting me was likewise faced with difficult to use systems and poor communication.

My experience in the healthcare system sparked a deeper interest in how technology can assist people navigating complex systems. I truly understood that design is "not just what it looks like and feels like. Design is how it works."

I've been dedicated to learning about user experience design and development ever since.

What I Do

I'm most passionate about visual storytelling, user-centered design, and trying to make the world a slightly better place. When I'm not working on my JavaScript skills, you'll find me at the gym training for my next powerlifting meet or at home doodling cartoon pugs.
